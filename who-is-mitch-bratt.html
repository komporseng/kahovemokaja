Who is Mitch Bratt? Meet the 19-year-old Canadian pitcher

If you are a fan of baseball, you might have heard of Mitch Bratt, the 19-year-old Canadian pitcher who is turning heads with his impressive performance in the minor leagues.

Bratt, who was drafted by the Texas Rangers in the fifth round of the 2021 MLB draft, has been dominating hitters with his fastball, curveball, and changeup.

He has also been representing his country on the international stage, as he was selected to play for Team Canada in the World Baseball Classic in 2023.

From Newmarket to Texas

Bratt was born and raised in Newmarket, Ontario, where he played baseball and hockey.

He attended Newmarket High School and also participated in various travel teams and showcase events.

He moved to Georgia in 2020 to join Georgia Premier Academy, a baseball training facility that gave him more exposure and opportunities.

Bratt pitched against college teams and other elite prospects and also played for the West Virginia Black Bears of the MLB Draft League.

He signed with the Rangers for $800,000 in 2021, which was above his slot value.

Rising Through the Ranks

Bratt made his pro debut in 2021 with the ACL Rangers of the Rookie-level Arizona Complex League.

He did not allow an earned run and struck out 13 batters in six innings.

Bratt moved up to Low-A Down East Wood Ducks of the Carolina League in 2022, where he had a 5-5 record with a 2.45 ERA and 99 strikeouts over 80 2/3 innings.

He won the Carolina League Pitcher of the Week award and the Wayne Norton Award as the top Canadian MiLB player.

In 2023, he was promoted to High-A Hickory Crawdads of the South Atlantic League.

He started strong but suffered a minor injury that sidelined him for a few weeks.

He returned in June and continued to dominate hitters with his command and poise.

Representing Canada on the International Stage

Bratt was selected to play for Team Canada in the World Baseball Classic in March 2023.

He faced Team USA in his first start, where he struggled against their powerful lineup.

However, he showed his potential and competitiveness on the big stage.

He is proud of his Canadian roots and hopes to inspire other young players from his country.

A Bright Future Ahead

Bratt is currently ranked as the No. 8 prospect in the Rangers organization by MLB.com.

He is projected to reach the majors by 2025 or 2026, depending on his development and performance.

He has been compared to fellow Canadian left-hander James Paxton, who has pitched for the Mariners and the Yankees.

Bratt has a similar build and repertoire as Paxton, but he also has his own style and personality.
