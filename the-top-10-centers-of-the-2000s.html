The Top 10 Centers of the 2000s

The 2000s saw the emergence of some of the most dominant and skilled centers in the history of basketball. These players not only showcased remarkable individual talent but also made significant contributions to their teams.

1. Shaquille O'Neal

Dominance in the Paint: Shaquille O'Neal, also known as Shaq, was a force to be reckoned with in the paint. His combination of size, strength, and agility made him nearly unstoppable close to the basket.

He led the Los Angeles Lakers to three consecutive NBA championships from 2000 to 2002 and was a dominant force throughout the decade.

2. Tim Duncan

Fundamental Excellence: Tim Duncan's exceptional skill set and fundamental understanding of the game made him one of the most reliable and consistent centers of the 2000s.

His leadership and on-court presence played a pivotal role in the San Antonio Spurs' success, leading them to multiple championships during this era.

3. Yao Ming

Global Impact: Yao Ming's arrival in the NBA brought an international spotlight to the league. The 7-foot-6 center from China possessed remarkable skill and footwork for a player of his size.

Despite injury setbacks, Yao Ming's impact on the game and the globalization of the NBA cannot be overstated.

4. Dwight Howard

Defensive Prowess: Dwight Howard emerged as a dominant defensive force in the 2000s. His shot-blocking ability and rebounding prowess earned him multiple Defensive Player of the Year awards.

5. Ben Wallace

Undersized Dominance: Despite being undersized for a traditional center, Ben Wallace's tenacity and defensive prowess made him a standout player in the 2000s.

His rebounding and shot-blocking abilities, combined with his leadership on the court, played a crucial role in the Detroit Pistons' championship run in 2004.

6. Amare Stoudemire

Athleticism and Scoring Ability: Amare Stoudemire's explosive athleticism and scoring ability made him a formidable offensive threat at the center position.

His partnership with Steve Nash on the Phoenix Suns' "Seven Seconds or Less" teams showcased his ability to dominate in an up-tempo system.

7. Alonzo Mourning

Resilience and Defensive Prowess: Alonzo Mourning's tenacity and defensive prowess defined his impact as a center in the 2000s.

Despite overcoming serious health issues, Mourning continued to be a defensive anchor for the Miami Heat, earning multiple All-Defensive Team selections during the decade.

8. Brad Miller

Versatility and Passing Prowess: Brad Miller's versatility and exceptional passing skills set him apart as a center in the 2000s.

His ability to facilitate the offense from the center position and stretch the floor with his shooting made him a valuable asset to the teams he played for, including the Sacramento Kings and the Chicago Bulls.

9. Marcus Camby

Defensive Anchor: Marcus Camby's shot-blocking and rebounding abilities made him a defensive stalwart during the 2000s.

His impact on the defensive end of the floor earned him the Defensive Player of the Year award in 2007, solidifying his status as one of the premier centers of the era.

10. Zydrunas Ilgauskas

Consistent Scoring and Rebounding: Zydrunas Ilgauskas' consistent scoring and rebounding made him a reliable presence at the center position.

His ability to score in the post and crash the boards earned him recognition as one of the top centers of the 2000s, particularly during his tenure with the Cleveland Cavaliers.

 

The 2000s showcased a remarkable array of talent at the center position, with each player contributing in their own unique way to the game of basketball.

From dominant forces in the paint to versatile playmakers, these centers left an enduring legacy on the sport and continue to influence the next generation of basketball players.
